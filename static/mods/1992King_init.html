Lore

Pre-1992

April 4, 1968: Martin Luther King Jr survives the assassination attempt, similar to how Ronald Reagan did in 1981.

1968-1972: MLK continues his activist career, and shifts towards economic justice and ending the Vietnam War.

November 7, 1972: Martin Luther King Jr gets elected to Georgia's 5th Congressional District, replacing retiring Republican Congressman Fletcher Thompson. King defeats Rodney Mims Cook Sr (R) by 10 percentage points, and begins a twenty-year long career in the United States House of Representatives. Martin Luther King Jr, alongside Barbara Jordan in Texas, became the first African-Americans elected to Congress from the former Confederacy since Reconstruction.

1973-1993: During Mr. King's time in Congress, he fights to make his dream a reality, working on behalf of his fellow Black brothers and sisters, other racial minorities, workers, people with disabilities, and other groups that Republican politicians have neglected. What about Wyche Fowler? Didn't he represent GA-5? In this timeline, he would be Mayor of Atlanta from 1982-1986 instead of Andrew Young before successfully running for Senate in 1986.

1992 Presidential Election



Atlanta Congressman Martin Luther King Jr and West Virginia Senator Jay Rockefeller defeat President George H.W. Bush and Vice President Dan Quayle with 317 electoral votes to Bush/Quayle's 221 electoral votes.

1992 Senate Elections




The only changes from our timeline see Geraldine Ferraro winning the NY Democratic primary and defeating Al D'Amato, and Wyche Fowler getting re-elected over Paul Coverdell. The Senate would see 58 Democrats and 42 Republicans at the start of King's term.

King's First Term

The King administration would be the first Democratic administration since fellow Georgian Jimmy Carter's presidency in the 1970s. It would also be the most liberal in decades. For those who know the Hope mod (one of my main inspirations for this timeline here), some major differences that MLK would make compared to Clinton include stopping NAFTA, passing George Mitchell's healthcare plan, vetoing Don't Ask, Don't Tell, and keeping troops out of Somalia and Bosnia - focusing on humanitarian aid only. He could potentially bring peace to Rwanda, but I'm not exactly sure.

King's Cabinet would not be too much different from Clinton's, but more liberal than anything. Al Gore would be Secretary of Energy, Tom Harkin would be HHS Secretary, and Robert Rubin would be Treasury Secretary from the jump. Jerry Brown and Jesse Jackson may be in the Cabinet at some position, but that is not relevant for the mod.

1994 Midterms

With the vacancies of Al Gore and Tom Harkin, Republicans would win both seats in the 1994 midterms. The Republican Revolution would still occur, though it would look somewhat different. The red wave would be stronger in the South, pushing Jeb Bush to the Governorship in Florida and Oliver North to the Virginia Senate seat. However, the Midwest and Northeast would be less susceptive to go red, allowing for Harris Wofford in Pennsylvania and Mario Cuomo in New York to survive. Furthermore, thanks to the President passing Senate Majority Leader Mitchell's healthcare plan, he would run again and win. There would also be a new Democratic appointee in West Virginia as Rockefeller vacates, but the seat would easily remain in Democratic hands. Richard Shelby and Ben Nighthorse Campbell also still switch to Republicans, creating a 52-48 Republican Senate. The House of Representatives would also go Republican, with Gingrich as Speaker as in our timeline.


1996 Presidential Election

Martin would not be quite as popular as Clinton was in our timeline, so he would have a tougher time getting re-elected. However, he would. The Republican Party found that Pete Wilson was the most electable candidate to topple the King administration, so they funded his campaign heavily and got him to the nomination. However, his voice would still be quite weak coming off his surgery, so he would leave former South Carolina Governor Carroll Campbell to do much of the work as his running-mate. It would no doubt be a fairly competitive race, but King and Rockefeller would get re-elected. Wilson would get nailed over Proposition 187, however King would not attack him on his lack of a voice. The King/Rockefeller ticket would win 308 electoral votes to Wilson and Campbell's 220, flipping the state of New Hampshire while losing Martin's home state of Georgia to Wilson.
