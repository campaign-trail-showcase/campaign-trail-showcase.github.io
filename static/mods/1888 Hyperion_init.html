The 1888 Presidential Election

Blair

The primary source for Blair's life was McKinney's Henry W. Blair's Campaign to Reform America: From the Civil War to the U.S. Senate (2013). It's pretty recent, thorough, and it contributed to the way I thought about the different currents of reform in American politics at the time. The best general overview of the Blair bill, in all its iterations, is Jenkins and Peck's \"The Blair Education Bill: A Lost Opportunity in American Public Education\" (2016), which describes its various forms, its potential impacts, and the coalitions for and against it. The bill's relationship with civil rights for freedman is discussed in Crofts' \"The Black Response to the Blair Education Bill\" (1971), and a look at the bill from the perspective of one of the poorest states can be found in \"North Carolina and Federal Aid to Education: Public Reaction to the Blair Bill, 1881-1890\" (1963). Finally, I learned about Blair's sometimes-conspiratorial relationship with Catholicism in \"Catholics and the Blair Education Bill\" (1960).

Vilas

Vilas has not inspired as much modern scholarly interest as the other two candidates, and as such the source I used for his life was the solid, if dry, biography William Freeman Vilas, Doctrinaire Democrat (1954), which covers how Vilas fused his traditional Democratic concern for the common man with his high-minded beliefs about material progress. I also drew from a 1912 compilation of his speeches, which frequently used Greek symbolism.

Gresham

I used Gilded Age Cato (2014) as the main source for Gresham's life, which organized his ideology into a decade-by-decade fight against the enemies he saw as corrupting America, and I supplemented this with Tyner's biographical 1933 article. The 1892 Omaha Platform details the original principles of the Populists. If you're interested in delving deeper into the real Populists, Lawrence Goodwyn's The Populist Moment provides an exhaustive account of the whole movement and its attempts at building coalitions across the diverse South and West.

Other

One interestng article that guided my thinking was Aneja and Guo's 2024 article \"Strengthening State Capacity: Civil Service Reform and Public Sector Performance during the Gilded Age\", which evaluated the efficiency of the post office before and after reform, finding that civil service reform beginning in the 1880s not only reduced postal errors, but also had the consequence of weakening local patronage politics across the country.

Finally, I referenced back to the 1889 biography of Conkling I've used in the previous entries.
